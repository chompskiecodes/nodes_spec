# EXPRESSIVE THERAPY KNOWLEDGE BASE

## What Expressive Therapy Is
Uses the performative arts — drama, music, song, storytelling — as therapeutic vehicles. Particularly powerful for children who find verbal therapy difficult or disengaging.

## Modalities at Acua
- Drama Therapy: Safe creative space to practice therapeutic goals through performance. Builds confidence, positive self-concept, self-esteem, resilience, and life skills.
- Art Therapy: For clients with difficulty expressing thoughts, fears, or trauma verbally. Develops self-control, self-empowerment, and confidence.
- Active Therapy: Stretching, movement, balance, and light activity during sessions. Many children are kinaesthetic — moving while in therapy is an effective conduit. Also contributes to physical wellbeing.
- Voice Production: Beyond speech therapy — communication confidence for presentations, public speaking, performance, media, podcasting, accents.

## Common Q&A
Q: "Is this play therapy?" → "Play therapy is one element — expressive therapy is broader. It draws on drama, art, movement, and storytelling as active therapeutic tools."
Q: "My child doesn't like to talk — is this right for them?" → "That's exactly the kind of child expressive therapy is designed for — it removes the pressure to verbalise."
Q: "Do therapists have performing arts backgrounds?" → "Some do — Acua looks for therapists with both education and creative backgrounds to bring this approach to life."
