## What Makes MRI First Different
MRI First uses an open, upright multi-position MRI system (0.6 Tesla, image quality comparable to a 1.5 Tesla lying-down MRI). Key advantages:

- **No claustrophobia**: Open design, no enclosed tunnel. Particularly helpful for children and people with claustrophobia.
- **No ionising radiation**: Uses magnetic fields and radio waves, not X-ray or CT-style radiation. Safe for all patients.
- **Upright and weight-bearing imaging**: Patients can be scanned sitting, standing, bending, or in positions that reproduce their pain, revealing sources of pain and dysfunction that lying-down scans may miss.
- **Multi-position capability**: Scans can be taken during movement or in pain-trigger positions for more accurate diagnosis.
- **Patient-first environment**: Relaxed, open environment with entertainment (TV) available during scans.

### Scan positioning by body part
- Head, spine, shoulder, hands: seated position on a chair
- Pelvis, hips, legs: standing, rotated at a slight angle (not flat)
- Elbow: lying flat, on the side (the only scan done flat)
- About 95% of all scans are done upright

## Billing, Medicare & Private Health
- MRI First does not offer bulk billing or Medicare rebates
- Reason: to be eligible for Medicare, a clinic must also offer CT scans; MRI First is an MRI-only clinic, so it doesn't qualify
- Some private health funds may offer partial reimbursement — patients should check directly with their provider

## Scan Duration
- Most individual scans take roughly 18–30 minutes of active scan time depending on body part (e.g. neck ~18 minutes, shoulder up to ~30 minutes)
- A ~20 minute buffer is built into scan times for patient comfort
- Generally allow up to 1 hour total for a visit, including preparation and any positional adjustments
- Each scan is broken into 2–4 minute segments; stillness is required only during those segments. Calm, relaxed breathing is fine — big or rapid breaths reduce image quality. Motion during a segment means it needs to be repeated, which extends scan time.

## Machine Specs & Patient Fit
- Upright MRI, 0.6 Tesla — image quality comparable to a 1.5 Tesla lying-down MRI
- Maximum weight capacity: 180kg
- The machine is narrower than a standard lying-down MRI — patients will feel some contact on the shoulders, which most people stop noticing after a minute or two, especially since they're upright, unobstructed, and can watch TV
- For patients over 140kg, this tightness can increase depending on shoulder width — patients with concerns can be offered a call with an operator

## What to Expect
- The only scan involving something over the face is a head scan, which requires a head cage (standard for any MRI, anywhere). MRI First's head cage is 2–3x the size of a standard lying-down machine's cage, giving plenty of visibility and room to watch TV during the scan.
- Spine scans capture the spine in an upright, weight-bearing position and while bending forwards/backwards — useful for identifying chronic pain origins that a lying-down scan can miss.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do you bulk bill?**
No. MRI First is a private, MRI-only clinic. To qualify for Medicare bulk billing, a clinic must also offer CT scans, which MRI First does not.

**Can I claim anything back from Medicare or private health?**
No Medicare rebate applies. Some private health funds may offer partial reimbursement — check with your provider.

**How much does a scan cost?**
$600 for the first scan, $300 for each additional scan on the same day.

**How long is the wait for an appointment?**
Usually fast — same week or the following week, depending on availability.

**Can I sit or stand during the scan?**
Yes. The system supports seated and standing weight-bearing imaging, and patients can bend into pain-trigger positions so the scan captures the problem area in its natural state.

**How long does a scan take?**
Individual scan segments run a few minutes each; total active scan time is roughly 18–30 minutes depending on body part, with allowance for up to 1 hour total per visit.

**Can I move during the scan?**
Not during each 2–4 minute scan segment — stillness is needed then. Calm breathing is fine; movement means that segment has to be repeated.

**What Tesla/strength is your MRI?**
0.6 Tesla, with image quality comparable to a 1.5 Tesla lying-down MRI.

**Is there anything over my face?**
Only for head scans, which require a head cage (standard across all MRI providers). MRI First's cage is 2–3x larger than a standard lying-down machine's, with clear visibility and TV access during the scan.

**What's the heaviest patient you can accommodate?**
Up to 180kg. The machine is narrower than most, so patients will feel some contact on the shoulders — this typically eases after a minute or two. For patients over 140kg, this tightness can increase depending on shoulder width.

**Why choose MRI First over a traditional MRI?**
No radiation (safer than CT/X-ray), no claustrophobic tunnel (open design), upright and weight-bearing imaging that captures the body in the position where pain is actually present, and a patient-first experience.

**Are upright MRI scans safe for everyone?**
Yes — MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves, no ionising radiation. Particularly beneficial for patients with claustrophobia or difficulty lying down.

## Referral and Patient Preparation
Before your scan:
- Bring your referral and any forms you were given to fill out
- Walk through the metal detector before your scan
- MRI uses a strong magnet — you must not have a pacemaker or certain implanted devices or prostheses
- Remove all metal objects: keys, coins, jewellery, hairpins, watches, phones, bank cards
- If you have a prosthesis, pacemaker, other implanted device, or think you may need sedation, contact the clinic before your appointment
