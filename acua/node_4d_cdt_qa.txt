# CDT KNOWLEDGE BASE

## What CDT Is
A unique intervention designed by Acua. A subset of behaviour therapy that integrates Education and Counselling — blending educational structure and pedagogy with therapeutic intervention to address cognitive development delay.

## The "Puzzle Piece" Metaphor
If you picture the brain as a puzzle, some pieces may not be clicked in correctly, fully formed, or may be missing. CDT works to address those missing or incomplete pieces.

## What CDT Addresses
Recognition and recall, memory and working memory, mind-mapping, comprehension, discrimination, pattern recognition, sequential / non-sequential / conceptual information processing, ideation, order, and categorisation.

## CDT vs Standard Schooling
Acua's approach addresses the challenges of the standard sequential curriculum. The "conceptual information synthesis" model meets children where they are rather than forcing a sequential track that doesn't suit their processing style.

## CDT vs Behaviour Therapy
CDT is a subset of behaviour therapy. Behaviour therapy focuses on emotional regulation and social development; CDT focuses specifically on cognitive and learning development. Some children benefit from both.

## Common Q&A
Q: "Is this like tutoring?" → "Not quite — CDT is therapeutic intervention, not academic tutoring. It addresses the underlying cognitive processes that make learning difficult."
Q: "My child has been assessed — does that change anything?" → "An existing assessment is really useful background for the therapist — share it and the team can factor it into planning."
Q: "Can CDT help with ADHD-type symptoms?" → "Attention and processing challenges are common areas CDT addresses — the therapist will explore the specifics at the intake."
Q: "How long until we see improvement?" → "Every child is different. The therapist will discuss realistic expectations after the intake."
