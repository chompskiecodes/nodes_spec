# Palm Beach Osteopathy

## ABOUT

Palm Beach Osteopathy is a locally owned clinic in Palm Beach, Gold Coast, offering osteopathic care, remedial massage, and red light therapy. Appointments are available throughout the week, including early morning and late afternoon sessions.

## OSTEOPATHY

### About Osteopathy

Osteopathy is a drug-free, hands-on therapy that focuses on how muscles, joints, nerves, and connective tissues work together to support movement, reduce pain, and improve overall wellbeing. Treatment is tailored to each patient's body and goals.

### What to Expect

#### Case History

Your osteopath will take a detailed history of your presenting complaint, past medical history, family history, medications, and lifestyle habits.

#### Assessment

Your osteopath will move and test your body to see how joints, muscles, and tissues are working together to understand what is contributing to your discomfort.

#### Treatment

A mix of hands-on techniques tailored to address symptoms and underlying causes. These may include soft tissue massage, joint articulation and manipulation, dry needling, myofascial cupping, and shockwave therapy.

#### Take-Home Advice

Your osteopath will provide personalised exercises, stretches, posture tips, breathing techniques, ergonomic advice, and other practical tools to support recovery between appointments.

### Conditions Treated

#### Musculoskeletal and Joint Issues

Shoulder, elbow, hip, knee, and foot pain. Frozen shoulder and bursitis. Ligament sprains, muscle strains, and imbalances. Back, neck, rib, and jaw pain. Scoliosis, whiplash, and workplace-related injuries. Overuse injuries and sports injuries.

#### Pain and Chronic Conditions

Chronic pain and fibromyalgia. Headaches and migraines. Jaw pain and teeth grinding (bruxism). Vertigo and concussion.

#### Post-Operative and Recovery Support

Post-operation rehabilitation. General maintenance and stress-related dysfunction. Swelling and inflammation.

#### Functional and Systemic Health

Breathing dysfunctions and respiratory issues. Digestive issues. Immune system support. Nervous system regulation.

### Treatment Techniques

#### Osteopathic Manipulation

Osteopaths use articulation, strain-counterstrain, joint manipulation, functional technique, muscle energy technique (MET), soft tissue massage, and stretching and strengthening techniques.

#### Dry Needling

Trigger-point dry needling inserts a fine, single-use needle into a muscle to release myofascial trigger points and tight muscles, reducing pain and spasm and increasing range of motion.

#### Myofascial Cupping

Suction cups placed onto the skin target underlying fascia, muscles, and fluids to address muscle tension and promote circulation.

#### Craniosacral Therapy

A gentle hands-on technique using light touch to examine membranes and fluid movement around the central nervous system to calm the nervous system.

#### Lymphatic Drainage

Helps remove waste materials from compromised areas to restore healthy metabolic and immune function by first addressing structures that impede lymphatic flow.

#### Visceral Manipulation

Hands-on technique encouraging normal mobility, tone, and motion of internal organs and connective tissues.

#### Kinesiology Taping

Thin, stretchy tape applied to the skin to provide functional support, manage injuries, support weak areas, re-educate muscles, and assist with decreasing pain and swelling.

#### Exercise Prescription

Targeted activation, strengthening, and stretching exercises prescribed during treatment to continue progress between appointments.

#### Shockwave Therapy

Acoustic waves applied to targeted areas to promote healing and tissue regeneration, enhance blood flow, and reduce pain and inflammation.

## FAQ

#### Do I need a referral from my GP?

No referral is required. Osteopaths are primary care practitioners. If you have a chronic medical condition requiring complex care, you may be eligible for an Enhanced Primary Care (EPC) plan requiring a special GP referral form.

#### What do I need to bring?

Bring any x-rays, scans, or test results you may have.

#### Can I bring a friend or relative?

Yes, someone can be present throughout your consultation and treatment.

#### Do I need to arrive early?

You will need to fill out an initial patient form online before your appointment. This will be sent by email once your booking is made. If you cannot fill it out online, arrive 15 minutes early to complete it at the clinic.

#### What should I wear?

Wear a comfortable shirt and loose pants or shorts. Depending on the area requiring treatment, your osteopath may ask you to undress to your underwear. Gowns are available.

#### How long will my appointment take?

Initial consultations are 45 minutes. Follow-up treatments are 30 minutes.

#### How many appointments will I need?

This depends on your condition. Generally some change in symptoms occurs after one or two visits. Long-term or chronic conditions may require more treatment. Your osteopath will discuss this with you.

#### Is osteopathic treatment painful?

Most osteopathic treatment is gentle. If your injuries require treatment of painful areas, your osteopath will aim to make you as comfortable as possible. You may experience mild soreness for a day or two after treatment, similar to mild exercise.

#### Can I claim Osteopathy on private health insurance?

Yes. Different insurers pay different amounts and some policies may not cover you. Check with your insurer about your rebate. Your health fund rebate is paid directly to the clinic via HICAPS, leaving you to pay only the gap on the day.

#### What conditions might benefit from visceral manipulation?

Abdominal pain, bloating, constipation, cramps, indigestion, chronic spinal dysfunction, whiplash, headaches and migraines, peripheral joint pain, sciatica, swallowing dysfunctions, nausea and acid reflux, gastritis, chronic pelvic pain, endometriosis, bladder incontinence, prostate dysfunction, and pudendal neuralgia.

#### What is the difference between dry needling and acupuncture?

Both use fine needles inserted into specific locations. Dry needling targets trigger points in tight muscles based on Western medical principles. Acupuncture inserts needles along meridian lines based on ancient Chinese medicine.

#### How are osteopaths different to physiotherapists and chiropractors?

Osteopaths focus on the health of the entire body as a whole unit. Chiropractors focus mainly on spinal alignment. Physiotherapists focus on exercise, movement, and rehabilitation.
