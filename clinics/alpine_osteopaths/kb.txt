# Alpine Osteopaths

## About the Clinic

Alpine Osteopaths blends expert hands-on treatments with exercise advice and lifestyle guidance to help patients recover from pain and injury. The clinic takes a holistic approach to health, addressing musculoskeletal issues through hands-on techniques, exercise prescription, and lifestyle advice. Osteopathic treatments restore balance, relieve discomfort, and support the body's natural healing processes.

## Our Process

#### Case History

During your first appointment, a detailed medical history is taken. Questions may cover matters that seem unrelated to current symptoms, but this thorough approach helps create a treatment plan that addresses both immediate symptoms and underlying contributing factors.

#### Assessment

Following the medical history, a physical examination is conducted. This may involve assessing posture, joint mobility, and muscle tension. The findings are explained clearly, including how your symptoms relate to your overall health and why specific treatments are recommended.

#### Treatment

A variety of hands-on techniques are used, tailored to individual needs. These may include gentle manipulation, stretching, massage, or other methods to improve mobility and reduce pain. Patients are kept informed and comfortable throughout treatment.

#### Management

After treatment, a plan for ongoing care is provided. This may include lifestyle change advice, exercises to support recovery, and recommendations for follow-up treatments, along with an expected timeline for improvement.

## Frequently Asked Questions

#### Do you accept Medicare rebates?

For patients with multiple or chronic conditions, a GP may refer them to an osteopath under the GPCCMP plan. This allows for a maximum of 5 visits per year, which may be shared between allied health providers. A rebate of $61.80 is available. Patients pay in full at the time of the appointment and Medicare provides the rebate once processed, usually within 24 hours. DVA referrals are also accepted.

#### What do I need to wear?

Wear loose, comfortable clothing that allows for movement. You may be asked to remove some clothing depending on the area being treated, with comfort and privacy always ensured.

#### How is osteopathy different from physiotherapy and chiropractic?

Osteopathy takes a holistic approach considering how the body's musculoskeletal system, including the spine, joints, muscles, and nerves, works together to affect overall wellbeing. In addition to hands-on techniques like stretching, massage, and manipulation, osteopaths assess lifestyle factors such as posture, activity levels, and stress. The aim is to promote long-term healing and enhance quality of life.

#### Is treatment painful?

Osteopathic treatment is generally not painful, though mild discomfort may be experienced, particularly with muscle tension or injury. Treatments are tailored to your comfort level and any discomfort is temporary. Let your osteopath know if you feel discomfort so adjustments can be made.

#### How many treatments will I need?

The number of treatments depends on your condition, its duration, your lifestyle, and overall health. Changes in symptoms may begin after one or two visits. Long-term or chronic conditions may require more treatments or ongoing management. Your osteopath will discuss your diagnosis and treatment plan after examination.
