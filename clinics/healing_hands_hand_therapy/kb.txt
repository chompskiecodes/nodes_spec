## CONDITIONS TREATED

#### Fractures and Dislocations
Hand therapy for fractures and dislocations of the fingers, hand, wrist, and elbow focuses on reducing pain, restoring movement, and supporting recovery through guided rehabilitation.

#### Hand Sprains and Soft Tissue Injuries
Sprains and soft tissue injuries in the hand can cause pain, swelling, and reduced function. Treatment focuses on controlled movement, swelling management, custom splinting, and progressive strengthening to restore hand use for daily tasks and work activities.

#### Tendon Repair (Post-Surgery)
Post-operative hand therapy follows evidence-based phases, beginning with protection and swelling management, and gradually progressing through guided early movement, strengthening, and a safe return to daily activities.

#### TFCC Injury (Triangular Fibrocartilage Complex)
Provides pain relief and wrist stability support for TFCC injuries. Evidence-based approach includes custom wrist splinting, graded mobilisation, and targeted strengthening to promote healing and restore function.

#### Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS)
Evidence-based techniques include custom splinting, nerve gliding exercises, pain relief strategies, and progressive strengthening to reduce symptoms and restore hand function.

#### Hand and Wrist Arthritis
Evidence-based arthritis therapy includes custom splinting, joint protection techniques, pain management strategies, and progressive exercises designed to improve hand function and support daily activities.

#### Dupuytren's Contracture
Dupuytren's contracture causes thickening and tightening of tissue in the palm, limiting finger movement. Treatment focuses on improving finger mobility, reducing stiffness, and supporting hand function through custom splints, stretching exercises, and scar management.

#### Ganglion Cysts
Ganglion cysts are fluid-filled lumps near joints or tendons in the hand or wrist. Conservative management includes custom splinting and activity modification to reduce pain and improve mobility. Post-surgical rehabilitation also provided to restore strength and function.

#### Trigger Finger (Stenosing Tenosynovitis)
Occurs when tendon inflammation narrows the tendon sheath, causing the finger to lock or catch. Treatment includes splinting, activity modification, anti-inflammatory modalities, and gentle stretching. Severe cases may require corticosteroid injections or surgery.

#### De Quervain's Syndrome
De Quervain's tenosynovitis causes pain and swelling at the base of the thumb, affecting tendons that control thumb movement. Treatment includes custom splinting, gentle stretching, strengthening exercises, and activity modification to reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and restore full thumb and wrist function.

#### Wounds, Scars, and Burns
Techniques include scar management, desensitisation, and compression therapy to reduce pain, soften scar tissue, and improve sensation. Promotes mobility, helps prevent hypertrophic scars and keloids, and restores optimal hand function throughout healing.

#### Tennis Elbow and Golfer's Elbow
Caused by overuse of forearm muscles and tendons. Treatment includes braces to reduce stress on affected tendons, combined with stretching, strengthening exercises, and activity modification to relieve pain, restore mobility, and improve elbow function.

#### Sports-Related Hand Injuries
Includes mallet finger, skier's thumb, jersey finger, fractures, sprains, and ligament tears. Treatment focuses on splinting, strengthening, joint mobilisation, and functional exercises to restore range of motion, stability, and a safe return to sport.

#### Digital Nerve Injuries
Can cause numbness, tingling, or loss of sensation in the fingers. Post-repair therapy focuses on scar management, sensory re-education, and functional retraining. Treatment includes splinting, gentle exercises, and desensitisation techniques to restore sensation and hand movement.

## TREATMENT SERVICES

### Custom Splinting
Custom-made and prefabricated/off-the-shelf braces designed to support healing after surgery, immobilise fractures, and protect vulnerable joints. Ensures optimal positioning, comfort, and function throughout rehabilitation.

### Post-Surgical Rehabilitation
Proven recovery protocols — managing swelling, wound care, supporting joints with splints, and guided exercises to rebuild strength and movement.

### Tendon and Nerve Rehabilitation
Early Active Mobilisation Protocols; Sensory Re-education and Desensitisation Techniques.

### Arthritis Management
Joint protection techniques and work simplification; splinting for pain relief and functional support; graded exercises to minimise joint load and improve function.

### Wound and Scar Care
Post-operative wound care and suture removal; scar massage; silicone therapy; compression therapy and desensitisation.

### Swelling and Oedema Management
Retrograde massage; elevation strategies; compression techniques.

### Overuse Injuries and Tendinopathies
Management of De Quervain's tenosynovitis, lateral epicondylitis, and trigger finger. Load management, taping, splinting, and exercise-based rehabilitation.

### Sports and Work-Related Injuries
Treatment for acute and overuse injuries of the hand, wrist, and forearm. Task-specific strengthening, splinting, and functional retraining. Graded return-to-sport and return-to-work programs.

## PATIENT INFORMATION

### Appointments
#### How long are consultations?
Initial appointment: approximately 1 hour. Arrive 10 minutes early. Bring referral (if applicable), Medicare card, and radiology reports — you can book without a referral. The first session includes an initial evaluation and discussion of the treatment plan.
Follow-up appointments: usually 30 minutes; complex conditions may need longer.

### Medicare and Insurance
#### Is hand therapy covered by Medicare?
Generally not. If you have a chronic medical condition, you may be eligible for a Chronic Disease Management Plan (EPC), entitling you to a Medicare rebate on up to 5 treatments per year. Ask your GP for an Occupational Therapy team care plan. 

#### Is there an EPC gap fee?
A gap fee applies: $89 for initial appointments and $59 for review (follow-up) appointments.

#### Will private health insurance cover appointments?
Depends on your health insurance level of cover. Contact your insurer to confirm coverage for Occupational Therapy and custom-made splints.

#### Are splints and consumables included in the consultation fee?
No. Custom-made splints, off-the-shelf splints, and other consumables are charged separately on top of the consultation fee.

#### Can I use both Medicare (EPC) and private health for the same appointment?
No. Medicare-funded plans and private health insurance cannot be combined for the same appointment.

### Payment
#### How do I pay?
Debit card, credit card, or cash. Afterpay, Zippay, Openpay, and other BNPL platforms are not offered. Health insurance claims can be processed through HICAPS; consumables are invoiced separately and may be claimable through your insurer.
