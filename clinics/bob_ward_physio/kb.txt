## SERVICES

### Physiotherapy

Physiotherapy is a science-based profession that takes a whole-person approach to health and wellbeing. Physiotherapists help people affected by injury, illness, or disability through movement and exercise, manual therapy, education, and advice.

The clinic follows a structured patient care pathway that takes patients from pain through to enhanced performance. This journey involves screening the way a patient moves to identify any movement faults that may have contributed to an injury, before gradually assisting return to performance whether that is work or sport.

Benefits of physiotherapy include managing pain, restoring normal movement and body function, improving general wellbeing, promoting independence, preventing recurrence of injury, enhancing performance, and reducing future injury risk.

Physiotherapy helps with the following conditions: neck pain, back pain, tendinopathy, joint pain, ligament sprains, whiplash, temporomandibular joint (TMJ) dysfunction, and cartilage tears.

During the initial physiotherapy assessment patients receive a thorough examination, a specific diagnosis, and clear goals aimed at meeting their requirements. Treatment techniques used may include hands-on treatment such as joint mobilisations, massage, and myofascial release; instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilisation (IASTM); bespoke exercise programmes via a mobile phone app; taping; self-mobilisation techniques such as foam roller, trigger point ball, and floss band; electrotherapy (Winback Tecartherapy); and a return-to-work plan.

### MSK Clinic — Shockwave Therapy

Shockwave therapy, also known as Extracorporeal Shockwave Therapy (ESWT), is a non-invasive treatment modality. It involves the application of acoustic waves to targeted areas of the body to stimulate tissue repair, promote healing, and relieve pain. The therapy works by delivering a shockwave of pressure at higher intensity than a Thera-gun. It was first approved by the FDA in the year 2000 for plantar fasciitis and has been used in elite sport for over 20 years.

Shockwave can be used to aid recovery from numerous injuries including achilles tendinopathy, hamstring injuries, rotator cuff problems, and tennis elbow. There is strong clinical evidence for a large number of conditions. Complications are considered more or less non-existent — while it is a somewhat painful treatment, there are no recorded complications that limit the healing process or cause lasting injury.

The treatment involves applying the shockwave using ultrasound gel, with pressure built up to approximately 2.5 bar and 2,000 to 4,000 pulses delivered over 10 minutes. The shockwave treatment is completed alongside a progressive loading protocol to ensure the best possible outcome. The minimum recommended course is 6 sessions, with 5 to 10 days between sessions. Most patients notice notable improvement in function and pain after 3 sessions. Some insurance providers cover Shockwave treatment.

### Injection Therapy

Injection therapy at The Bob Ward Physiotherapy Clinic is available with Mark Taylor. The clinic offers a range of clinically governed injection therapies designed to reduce pain, decrease inflammation, and support faster recovery from musculoskeletal conditions. Injection therapy can be an effective option when symptoms persist despite physiotherapy or conservative treatment. All injection treatments are performed following a full clinical assessment.

#### Traumeel Injections

A natural, homeopathic anti-inflammatory injection used to support the body's healing response. Traumeel can help reduce inflammation, relieve pain, and improve recovery in joints, muscles, and soft tissues.

#### Steroid (Corticosteroid) Injections

Used to reduce significant inflammation within joints, tendons, or bursae. Steroid injections can provide rapid relief of pain and swelling in conditions such as arthritis, tendonitis, and bursitis.

#### Botulinum Toxin (Botox) Injections

Used therapeutically to reduce muscle overactivity, relieve chronic muscle spasm, and assist with certain pain conditions by temporarily relaxing targeted muscles.

#### Hyaluronic Acid Injections

A joint lubrication therapy often used for osteoarthritis. Hyaluronic acid helps restore joint fluid viscosity, improving cushioning, movement, and pain levels within the joint. Many patients notice improvement within a couple of weeks, with long-lasting relief typically lasting 6 to 12 months.

#### Cortisone Injections

Powerful anti-inflammatory treatment used to relieve pain and reduce inflammation in affected joints or soft tissues. Often used for conditions such as shoulder pain, knee arthritis, plantar fasciitis, and tendon inflammation.

#### Anti-Inflammatory Injections

Targeted injections designed to reduce inflammation and pain in irritated joints or soft tissues, supporting improved mobility and rehabilitation.

#### Neurexan Injections

A homeopathic preparation used to help regulate the nervous system and support the management of stress-related symptoms, muscular tension, and nervous system imbalance.

#### Engystol Injections

Used to support the immune system and help the body respond to viral infections and immune stress. Engystol may assist in strengthening the body's natural defence mechanisms.

#### Vertigoheel Injections

A homeopathic preparation commonly used in the management of dizziness and vertigo-related symptoms. It supports balance mechanisms and neurological regulation.

### Sports Injuries and Vestibular Rehabilitation

The clinic's specialist physiotherapists are trained in Vestibular Rehabilitation Therapy (VRT) for patients suffering from dizziness, loss of balance, nausea, or confidence issues with balance. Vestibular problems may manifest as difficulty looking over the shoulder, rolling in bed, or managing tasks such as a weekly shop due to dizziness.

Vestibular physiotherapy includes a full medical history evaluation covering symptom frequency, impact, intensity and triggers; balance and movement analysis; examination of muscles and joints associated with symptoms; specific tests for the ears and eyes to determine the cause; hands-on treatment specific to the condition including the Epley manoeuvre, the Semont manoeuvre, and the reverse Epley; an individualised vestibular rehabilitation programme of specific exercises; discussion regarding self-management and future treatments; and referral to other specialties if required.

The package is suitable for runners, gym enthusiasts, team sport athletes, and mature athletes. It includes a 60-minute screening session, a 30-minute follow-up, professional physiotherapy expertise, and gym access.

### Sports Massage

Sports massage is the vigorous manipulation of soft tissues for the benefit of sports and exercise participants. All therapists at the clinic are qualified physiotherapists delivering sports massage to the highest standard. Their knowledge and ability to identify the root of a problem ensures they can meet all patient requirements. Sports massage is often used in conjunction with sports rehabilitation and sports screening to optimise the effect.

Sports massage is useful for maintenance (managing longer-term issues and addressing ongoing postural or repetitive strain problems), post-event recovery (accelerating recovery to avoid disruption to ongoing training), and pre-event preparation (ensuring muscles and soft tissue are supple and ready for activity).

### Acupuncture

Acupuncture at the clinic follows a Western medical approach, linking the science of anatomy and physiology to the needling of specific point locations within the body. Stimulation of muscle, nerve, and skin can reduce muscle tension, activate the body's pain-killing hormones, and increase blood flow. All therapists administering acupuncture are registered with the Acupuncture Association of Chartered Physiotherapists (AACP) and undergo regular CPD training.

Benefits of acupuncture include pain relief, increased blood flow, improved healing, reduced muscle tension, reduced swelling, and relaxation. Acupuncture can be carried out within a physiotherapy treatment following a physiotherapy assessment. It can also be offered as a stand-alone treatment. Sessions typically last approximately 20 to 30 minutes. All needles are individually packaged in sterile packaging.

### Ergonomics and Biomechanics (Workstation Assessments)

Ergonomic assessments can be carried out in person at the patient's workplace or remotely online, which is useful for homeworkers. Prior to the assessment patients complete a pre-assessment questionnaire. Assessments include review of the working environment and posture, a bespoke video exercise programme, advice on ergonomic equipment, and a summary report with recommendations. The clinic can also source and fit appropriate equipment such as monitor arms, ergonomic mice, laptop stands, and standing desks. Educational workplace seminars on posture and ergonomics lasting approximately 90 minutes are also available.

### Biomechanics and Footscan Assessment

Biomechanics play a large role in injury prevention and treatment by identifying and correcting imbalances in foot position, walking and running patterns, and lower limb deformity. The clinic uses a footscan assessment with state-of-the-art RS Scan foot scan equipment to assess both static and dynamic posture. Therapists analyse the scan findings and discuss appropriate treatment options, which may include a change of footwear or Phits 3D Printed Orthotics.

A common issue addressed is over-pronation, where the foot rolling inwards causes excessive rotation in the leg, pushing the knee inwards, which can lead to uneven pull on the kneecap and increased risk of knee pain, with knock-on effects at the hip and lower back. The clinic can also observe patients running on a treadmill, using video software to slow down and analyse running style.

Benefits include orthotics and insoles recommendations, running assessment where appropriate, footwear fitting and advice, objective examination, and footscan pressure plate assessment. This service is available for patients suffering from foot, ankle, knee, or hip pain.

### Winback Teckar Therapy

Winback Teckar Therapy is a high-frequency non-invasive energy treatment which stimulates the body's natural cellular ability to repair itself. It reduces pain and inflammation, restores movement, and increases blood flow.

### Compex Muscle Stimulation

Compex electric muscle stimulation can be added to training or rehabilitation to maximise muscular effort by engaging a greater percentage of muscle fibres.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

#### What is physiotherapy?

Physiotherapy is the treatment of injury, illness, and disability through movement and exercise. Physiotherapists are highly qualified healthcare professionals trained to assess, diagnose, and treat a wide range of conditions from sports injuries to osteoarthritis. All chartered physiotherapists at the clinic are members of the HCPC, which regulates all physiotherapists to ensure the highest quality of care.

#### What can I expect from my initial assessment?

A physiotherapy initial assessment lasts from 30 to 60 minutes depending on how services are accessed. Certain insurance providers allocate preset times, but self-funding customers are offered up to 60 minutes. The clinician collects information about the current condition as well as medical and social history. Patients who take medications should bring a prescription sheet or list of current medications. After a discussion, a thorough physical examination is undertaken, tailored to the individual based on pain level, physical ability, and comfort. An accurate diagnosis and bespoke treatment plan are then provided. Treatment always involves exercise as part of physical rehabilitation and can include spinal mobilisation and manipulation, IASTM, sports massage, acupuncture, and more.

#### How many sessions will I need?

The answer depends on many factors including physical ability, severity of the issue, and personal goals. An average course of physiotherapy tends to last anywhere from six to ten sessions.

#### Do I need to be referred by a doctor?

If funding the appointment personally, no GP referral is required — patients can self-refer directly. For certain insurance providers a GP referral may be required. If unsure, contact the clinic for guidance.

#### Which physiotherapist is best for me?

All physiotherapists are trained to deal with all aspects of musculoskeletal issues and are likely capable of dealing with any problem. The clinic regularly checks knowledge and holds monthly CPD sessions. Physiotherapist bios are available on the website, though these do not capture the full extent of each clinician's experience.

#### Are your clinics open for face-to-face appointments?

Yes. Face-to-face appointments are available at The Bob Ward Physiotherapy Clinic in Blackpool.

#### Will I always get treatment as part of my first session?

The initial session is primarily an assessment, but treatment is often provided at the same appointment where clinically appropriate.

#### What is a joint injection and why is it given?

A joint injection is a medical treatment in which medication is delivered directly into or around a joint to help reduce pain, inflammation, and stiffness. Joint injections are commonly used to treat conditions such as arthritis, tendonitis, bursitis, and other musculoskeletal injuries. They can improve joint movement, reduce swelling, and support rehabilitation alongside physiotherapy.

#### How soon will I feel better after a joint injection?

Some patients notice relief within a few days; for others it may take one to two weeks for the full effect to develop. There may be mild soreness at the injection site shortly after treatment, which usually settles quickly.

#### Can I work the next day after a joint injection?

In most cases, yes. It is generally recommended to rest the treated area for the first 24 hours and avoid heavy lifting, strenuous activity, or intense exercise.

#### Can I drive after a joint injection?

In most cases, yes, provided the patient feels comfortable and has full control of the treated area. If the injection was in a weight-bearing joint or the shoulder or arm used for steering, the clinician may advise avoiding driving for the rest of the day.

#### Do I need to rest after a joint injection?

It is generally recommended to rest the treated area for 24 to 48 hours and avoid strenuous activity or high-impact exercise involving the treated joint.

#### Can I have more than one injection at a time?

In some cases, yes, depending on the condition and clinical assessment. The clinician will discuss the most appropriate plan.

#### How many injections can I have in a year?

This depends on the type of injection and the condition. Corticosteroid injections are typically limited to a small number per year. The clinician will advise on the safest schedule.

#### Are there risks or side effects from injections?

Joint injections are generally considered safe when performed by a qualified clinician. Some patients experience temporary soreness, swelling, or mild discomfort at the injection site. In rare cases there may be infection, allergic reaction, or irritation of surrounding tissues. The clinician will discuss any potential risks beforehand.

#### What should I wear to an injection appointment?

Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing that allows easy access to the area being treated — for example, shorts for knee injections or a loose top for shoulder injections.

#### Will an injection hurt?

Most patients experience only mild discomfort. The procedure is usually quick, with a brief sting or pressure as the needle is inserted lasting only a few seconds. Some injections include a local anaesthetic. The treated area may feel slightly sore or tender for a day or two afterward.

#### Does shockwave therapy hurt?

Shockwave therapy is a somewhat painful treatment, though complications are considered more or less non-existent. There are no recorded complications that limit the healing process or cause lasting injury.
