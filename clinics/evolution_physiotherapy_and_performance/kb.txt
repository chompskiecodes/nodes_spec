# Evolution Physiotherapy and Performance

## ABOUT

### Our Approach
Evolution Physiotherapy and Performance is a sport and performance physiotherapy clinic in Heidelberg West, VIC. The clinic listens to each patient's history and concerns, provides a diagnosis, and guarantees a written treatment plan at the initial consultation. Treatment is active and hands-on, aimed at decreasing pain and improving movement as quickly as possible. Rather than only reducing symptoms, the clinic structures treatment to address the root cause of pain for long-term relief, aiming to avoid invasive procedures or unnecessary surgery where possible. Patients are educated on managing and resolving their own condition to reduce dependence on ongoing treatment. Once pain is under control, patients are set up with a structured strength training program to build long-term strength and resilience.

### Facilities and Service Area
The clinic has dedicated treatment rooms plus a purpose-built exercise and rehab gym with a range of equipment and floor space for everything from basic exercises to advanced strength progressions. Evolution Physiotherapy and Performance services the local community of Heidelberg West, Heidelberg Heights, Rosanna, Bellfield and surrounding suburbs.

## KNEE AND HIP OSTEOARTHRITIS PHYSIOTHERAPY

### About
The clinic treats knee and hip osteoarthritis, a whole-joint condition that can affect cartilage, ligaments, muscles and surrounding tissue. Common symptoms include reduced quality of life, morning or post-activity pain, joint stiffness and loss of range of motion, swelling and inflammation, and difficulty with tasks such as getting in or out of a chair or using stairs. Treatment includes structured exercise programs targeting pain relief, functional strength, and balance and coordination, plus group strength training classes, and the clinic accepts DVA, TAC and WorkCover patients for this program.

### GLA:D Program
GLA:D stands for "Good Life with osteoArthritis in Denmark" — an education and exercise program developed by Danish researchers for people with hip or knee osteoarthritis symptoms, brought to Australia in 2016. It runs as a 6-week program with 12 one-hour group exercise classes, twice weekly.

#### Program Structure
Participants start with an initial assessment appointment to explain the program and measure current functional ability. This is followed by two education sessions covering osteoarthritis, its risk factors, treatment options and self-management strategies, then the twice-weekly group exercise sessions for six weeks. A review at 3 months covers progress and goals and re-measures functional ability. Participants complete a questionnaire at registration and again at 3 and 12 months to track changes in pain, function, quality of life and medication use.

#### Benefits
Significant reduction in osteoarthritis symptom progression, pain level reductions of up to 32%, decreased use of pain medication, and stronger muscles with better balance and coordination.

#### Getting Started
Each participant books an initial assessment with a GLA:D-trained physiotherapist, who takes a thorough history, runs some simple tests and exercises, and answers questions before providing a personalised program.

## REMEDIAL MASSAGE THERAPY

### About
Remedial massage uses techniques such as deep tissue work, trigger point therapy and myofascial release to target problem areas caused by chronic conditions like back pain, neck pain or joint pain. It can help prevent injuries and speed recovery for active patients, and provide relief for muscle strains, tendonitis, sciatica or tension headaches, commonly in the lower back, neck, shoulders and legs. A typical session starts with a brief discussion of the problem area and a quick assessment so the therapist can tailor treatment. Therapists may liaise with the clinic's physio team if further examination or treatment is needed.

### Techniques Offered
Deep tissue massage, sports massage, and relaxation and recovery massage.

## STRENGTH TRAINING AND PERFORMANCE

### Strength Training
Strength training is offered to all patients, not just athletes, and can decrease pain, make everyday tasks easier, protect against injury, improve posture, and support mental health as well as physical health. Programs are customised to each patient's goals and current ability, and classes are kept small. Areas the clinic manages with strength training include acute and chronic pain, muscle tears and strains, acute musculoskeletal injuries, sporting injuries and injury prevention, longevity and healthy ageing, ACL injuries and ACL reconstruction surgery, knee pain and knee injuries, lower back or neck pain, and osteoarthritis.

### Strength and Conditioning
Strength and conditioning programming is exercise tailored to an individual's level to increase strength, endurance, power, muscular hypertrophy and cardiovascular fitness, run by clinicians qualified in program design and implementation for sport, recreational and everyday performance goals.

### ACL Rehabilitation
The clinic provides ACL rehabilitation for both pre- and post-surgical intervention and for non-surgical management, including return-to-sport testing criteria. The goal is a thorough recovery process that reduces the risk of ACL re-injury, particularly relevant since recurrence is high in athletes who return to sport too quickly or without a high limb symmetry index (LSI, target above 95%).

#### What's Included
Pre-surgical readiness, education and treatment; thorough post-surgery assessment and planning; injury education and rehab outlook; objective measures and specific rehab goals; milestone-centred, patient-specific programming; psychological readiness and preparation; education, tools and home programming; return-to-sport and physical activity readiness testing; evidence-based objective assessment; a choice of 1 or 2 sessions per week with home gym programs and education; and ongoing communication between the physio and the patient's medical team.

## REFERRALS

A referral is not required to see a physiotherapist at Evolution Physiotherapy and Performance. A referral or claim number is required at the initial appointment only when treatment is being claimed through WorkCover, DVA, TAC, or a GP care plan. Referring GPs and other providers (NDIS, private, DVA, TAC, WorkCover) can submit a referral through the referral form on the clinic website, which requests the referrer's and participant's details, plan details, funding category and primary diagnosis.

## FAQ

#### How do I book an appointment?
Appointments can be made by calling the clinic on 03 9020 3900, or online by clicking "book now" on the clinic's website to enter the booking portal.

#### What should I expect at my initial appointment?
A physiotherapist takes a detailed history of the presenting complaint and gathers relevant information to guide diagnosis and treatment. Patients receive a written treatment plan to resolve the issue, plus ongoing support throughout treatment.

#### What should I bring or wear to my appointment?
Comfortable clothing is recommended in case the physiotherapist needs to inspect the lower body (knees, hips, ankles) or upper body; shorts are available at the clinic to change into if needed. For an exercise class, bring a drink bottle and comfortable clothing to exercise in.

#### Do I need a referral?
No referral is needed to see a physiotherapist. A referral is only required if treatment is being claimed through WorkCover, DVA, TAC or a GP care plan — bring the claim number or care plan to the initial appointment to claim benefits.

#### Do you offer rebates?
Yes. Patients with a GP care plan can claim a rebate at their appointment; bring the referral.

#### Cancellation Policy
The clinic requires 24 hours' notice to reschedule or cancel an appointment. Failure to give 24 hours' notice incurs a fee of up to 100% of the consultation fee. Group class late cancellations or no-shows are subject to the same policy, or a session may be deducted from a pre-paid session pack. Consultation fees are payable on the day of service. Cancellations can be made by calling 03 9020 3900 or emailing info@evophysiotherapy.com.au.

#### Do you treat WorkCover, Veterans' Affairs and TAC patients?
Yes, the clinic accepts TAC, WorkCover and DVA claims and bills these directly once a claim number is provided. Payment is required on the day if claim acceptance has not yet been confirmed or the patient has reached their TAC, WorkCover or DVA medical threshold — in some cases a gap fee applies to cover the remainder of the consultation fee.

#### Can I use my private health cover at the clinic?
Yes. The clinic has HICAPS and EFTPOS facilities at reception for claiming extras cover rebates on the spot. If a patient doesn't have their health provider card at the time of consultation, the clinic provides receipt details so the rebate can be claimed afterwards. Cash, EFTPOS and credit card payments are all accepted.

#### Is there parking available?
Yes. On-street parking is available on Bell Street (either side), and off-street parking is also available on Edwin Street and surrounds.
