# OT KNOWLEDGE BASE

## What OT Is
Occupational therapy helps children develop, maintain, or recover the skills needed for daily life. It addresses physical and functional aspects to help a child engage fully in school, home, and social life.

## What Acua's OT Addresses
Fine motor skills (pencil grip, cutting, manipulation), gross motor skills (coordination, balance, movement), sensory processing (sensitivity, seeking, avoidance), daily living skills (dressing, feeding, self-care routines), handwriting, play skills, attention / ADHD-type presentations, coordination and body awareness.

## Session Approach
Play therapy techniques are integrated for paediatric clients — sessions feel natural and engaging, not like therapy exercises.

## Common Q&A
Q: "Do I need a referral?" → "No formal referral is required to book an intake. A professional recommendation is useful background but not a barrier."
Q: "Can OT help with sensory issues?" → "Yes — sensory processing is a core area of occupational therapy."
Q: "My child has autism / ADHD — can OT help?" → "Many children with those diagnoses benefit significantly from OT. The therapist will look at specific functional goals."
Q: "What does a session look like?" → "Sessions are one hour, activity-based — play, movement, and specific skill-building tasks designed for the child's goals."
Q: "Will we get homework?" → "Often the therapist gives the family practical strategies or activities to try between sessions."
Q: "Can NDIS fund OT?" → "OT may qualify depending on the child's plan — Acua cannot guarantee it. NDIS makes the determination."
