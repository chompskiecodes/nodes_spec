# SPEECH THERAPY KNOWLEDGE BASE

## The Approach: Voice Production
Acua's speech therapy uses a Voice Production approach — historically used in performance, theatre, and broadcasting, applied to paediatric therapy. Engaging, practical, builds communication confidence through doing.

## The Six Domains
- Domain 1: Sounds and Phonics
- Domain 2: Words, articulation, and pronunciation
- Domain 3: Sentences, communication, conversation, expression, and comprehension
- Domain 4: Language and literacy — pronouns, verb tenses, narrative structure, reading and writing foundations
- Domain 5: Physiological impairment (e.g. cleft palate, tongue-tie) — domain of Speech Pathologists
- Domain 6: Neurological impairment (e.g. stroke, brain injury) — domain of Speech Pathologists

Acua's therapists work across Domains 1–4. If Domains 5–6 are indicated, the team will advise and refer.

## Common Q&A
Q: "What age do you work with?" → "Children from early childhood through to adolescence — the approach adapts to the child's stage."
Q: "Will the school need to be involved?" → "Domain 4 overlaps with what schools are teaching, so some collaboration with the teacher may be recommended."
Q: "Is this speech pathology?" → "Our therapists work across communication and language domains — not physiological or neurological conditions. If a speech pathologist is indicated, the team will advise."
Q: "What does a session look like?" → "Sessions are one hour — active and engaging, not just talking. Play is woven in for younger children."
