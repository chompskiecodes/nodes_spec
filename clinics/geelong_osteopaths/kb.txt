## WHAT OSTEOPATHS TREAT — OVERVIEW

### About Osteopathy

Osteopaths diagnose and treat many musculoskeletal problems. If it's pain or discomfort and you feel it, we may be able to help. If we can't, we know the fastest way to get you to appropriate care.

### Conditions Treated

Geelong Osteopaths treats the following conditions:
- Headaches
- Neck Pain
- Back Pain
- Shoulder Pain
- Elbow Pain
- Wrist Pain
- Hip Pain
- Knee Pain
- Ankle Pain

---

## CONDITIONS IN DETAIL

### Headaches
- Muscular headaches (jaw clenching, trapezius, suboccipital tension)
- Nerve-related headaches (upper spine nerve entrapment)
- Joint-related headaches (upper neck C1/C2, temporomandibular/TMJ jaw joint)
- Vascular headaches
- Stress-related headaches
- Cervicogenic headaches (from neck stiffness)

### Neck Pain
- Muscular neck pain
- Joint stiffness and degeneration
- Disc degeneration or prolapse
- Whiplash and trauma
- Nerve compression
- Degenerative change

### Back Pain
- Muscular back pain
- Facet joint and rib-vertebra joint pain
- Disc (degenerative or prolapse)
- Ligament strains
- Nerve compression
- Referred pain from organs (reproductive, stomach, gall bladder, heart, lungs)

### Shoulder Pain
- Shoulder joint pain
- Referred pain from neck (disc)
- Rotator cuff (muscular tension or tear)
- Ligament strains
- Bursitis
- Rib joint pain
- Referred pain from organs (gall bladder, heart, lungs)

### Elbow Pain
- Golfers Elbow (medial epicondylitis)
- Tennis Elbow (lateral epicondylitis)
- Olecranon Bursitis
- Biceps Tendonitis
- Triceps Tendonitis
- Ulnar Nerve Pain

### Wrist Pain
- Carpal tunnel syndrome
- Tenosynovitis
- Tendon pain
- Scaphoid fracture
- Joint degeneration (thumb/trapezium)
- Nerve pain (from lower back or compression along the pathway)

### Hip Pain
- Trochanteric bursitis (side hip pain)
- Sciatica (referred from lower back)
- Labral issues (front hip)
- Glute muscle pain (back hip)
- Ligament pain
- Joint and tendon pain

### Knee Pain
- Ligament injuries (collateral or cruciate/ACL/PCL)
- Meniscus injuries
- Patellofemoral (kneecap) degeneration
- Arthritis
- Bursitis (infrapatellar, prepatellar, pes anserine)
- ITB friction syndrome
- Quadriceps/patellar tendonitis
- Osgood-Schlatter disease (adolescents)
- Referred pain from hip or lower back

### Ankle Pain
- Ligament sprains
- Achilles bursitis
- Tendonitis
- Nerve pain (from lower back)
- Joint dysfunction (biomechanics, footwear)
- Fractures
