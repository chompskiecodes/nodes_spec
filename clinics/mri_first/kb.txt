## What Makes MRI First Different

MRI First uses an open, upright multi-position MRI system. Key advantages:

- **No claustrophobia:** The open design eliminates the enclosed tunnel found in traditional MRI. Patients are not enclosed. This is particularly helpful for children and people with claustrophobia.
- **No ionising radiation:** MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves — no X-ray or CT-style radiation. Safe for all patients.
- **Upright and weight-bearing imaging:** Patients can be scanned sitting, standing, bending, or in positions that reproduce their pain. This reveals the true source of pain and dysfunction that lying-down scans may miss.
- **Multi-position capability:** Scans can be taken during movement or in pain-trigger positions for more accurate diagnosis.
- **Patient-first environment:** Relaxed, open environment with entertainment available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do you bulk bill?**
No. MRI First is a private clinic and does not offer bulk billing. Pricing is transparent — what you see is what you pay.

**Can I claim anything back from Medicare or private health?**
Medicare rebates do not apply. Some private health funds may offer partial reimbursement — check with your provider.

**Can I sit or stand during the scan?**
Yes. The system allows multi-position imaging including seated and standing weight-bearing scans. Patients can also bend into positions that trigger pain so the scan captures the problem area in its natural state.

**How long does a scan take?**
Most individual scans take 20–45 minutes depending on the study. Allow up to an hour including preparation and any positional adjustments.

**Can I move during the scan?**
Not during each scan segment. Each scan is broken into 2–4 minute segments — stillness is required only during those segments. Calm, relaxed breathing is fine; big or rapid breaths reduce image quality.

**Why choose MRI First over a traditional MRI?**
No radiation (safer than CT/X-ray), no claustrophobic tunnel (open design), upright and weight-bearing imaging to capture the body in the position where pain is actually present, patient-first experience.

**Are upright MRI scans safe for everyone?**
Yes. MRI uses magnetic fields and radio waves — no ionising radiation. Particularly beneficial for patients with claustrophobia or difficulty lying down.

## Referral and Patient Preparation

Before your scan:
- Bring your referral and any forms you were given to fill out.
- Walk through the metal detector before your scan.
- MRI uses a strong magnet — you must not have a pacemaker or certain implanted devices or prostheses.
- Remove all metal objects: keys, coins, jewellery, hairpins, watches, phones, bank cards.
- If you have a prosthesis, pacemaker, other implanted device, or think you may need sedation, contact the clinic before your appointment.

Contact before attending: Phone 0416 358 468 or email info@mrifirst.au.

## Clinical Studies and Research

MRI First conducts ongoing clinical research in upright MRI imaging. Current research areas:
- **CSF Flow:** Impact of upright positioning on cranial pressure and cerebrospinal fluid flow dynamics.
- **Spinal Stability Study:** Weight-bearing spinal alignment and chronic pain origins in active patients.
- **Pediatric Tolerance:** Anxiety reduction and procedural success in children using the upright MRI system.

Participation in studies usually requires a referral or meeting specific clinical criteria. Most studies are sponsored — no cost to participants for research scans. Contact the clinic to check eligibility.
