# BEHAVIOUR THERAPY KNOWLEDGE BASE

## The Approach
Talk therapy as the primary tool — exploring concepts and addressing behaviours in a safe therapeutic relationship. With younger children, play therapy techniques are integrated to build engagement and rapport.

## What It Addresses
Self-care, self-management, learning difficulty, socialisation, emotional regulation, anxiety, tension, depression, grief, stress, and behavioural challenges.

## Therapeutic Framework
Humanist, client-led. CBT and talk therapy as core modalities, complemented by narrative therapy, play therapy, and drama therapy. Multiple theoretical frameworks (gestalt, constructivist, and others) depending on the child.

## Team / Moderation
Therapists meet regularly to discuss clients' progress. The child benefits from the whole team's thinking. With paediatric clients, more than one therapist may be present in a session.

## Common Q&A
Q: "Is this like CBT?" → "CBT is one of the tools our therapists use — they draw on a range of approaches depending on what fits the child."
Q: "What does a session look like?" → "Sessions are one hour. For younger children, more activity and play-based. For older children and teens, more conversational."
Q: "Do you work with anxiety?" → "Yes — anxiety is one of the most common areas we support."
Q: "Do parents sit in?" → "That varies by age — the therapist will discuss it with you at the intake."
Q: "How many sessions?" → "That depends on the child and their goals. The therapist will discuss a plan after the intake."
Q: "What about CDT?" → "CDT is a subset of behaviour therapy focusing specifically on cognitive and learning development — I can tell you more about that."
