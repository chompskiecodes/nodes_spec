Being a woman comes with constant change… Through our cycles, pregnancy, birth, postpartum and beyond.

Sometimes those changes bring symptoms we’re told are “
just part of being a woman
” - things like bladder leaks, pelvic pain, painful periods, pain during sex, pregnancy discomfort, or a sense that something doesn’t feel quite right in your body.

While these symptoms are
common,
they are not always
- and you don’t have to simply live with them.

Cascade Women’s Health provides warm, evidence-based women’s and pelvic floor physiotherapy, supporting women to feel informed, confident and supported at every stage of life.

You leak urine when you cough, laugh, run or jump

You’re pregnant and dealing with pelvic, back or hip pain

You’ve had a baby and want to safely return to exercise

Values

At Cascade Women’s Health, the way we work with you matters as much as what we do.

These are the core values that guide every appointment, every conversation, and every decision we make, so you feel safe, supported and understood from the moment you walk in the door.

Respect -
You’ll always be heard

I believe everyone deserves to be treated equally and with respect, no matter their background, age, sexuality or walk of life. I celebrate individuality and recognize that every woman’s experience is unique. When you visit my clinic room, your needs and preferences are respected, and your voice is central to conversations around your health. My clinical approach is rooted in treating everyone with dignity, acknowledging each women’s expertise about their own body, and collaborating to create a tailored plan that aligns with an individual’s values and lifestyle.

Compassion
- You’ll never feel rushed or dismissed

Compassion is at the heart of everything I do. I understand that discussing pelvic health can be daunting, and I’m here to create a safe, supportive space where women feel heard and understood. My goal is to walk alongside you on your journey, offering empathy and encouragement every step of the way. I believe that when you feel supported, you’re more empowered to take control of your health. Together, we’ll explore your concerns with kindness and patience, ensuring you never feel alone in your experience.

Connection -
Working with you as a partner in your care

Building a genuine connection with my clients is essential to fostering trust and understanding. I strive to create a welcoming atmosphere where my clients feel comfortable in sharing thoughts and feelings about their body and health. I value open communication, allowing us to work as a team together towards achieving your goals.

Education -
You’ll leave with knowledge and confidence

Once you’ve had an appointment with me, you’ll see how passionate I am about empowering women with knowledge about their bodies! Knowledge is power, and I aim to help you understand your body and your condition including how to self-manage, especially longer term.. so you don’t have to rely on ongoing appointments for forever and a day!

I’m also deeply committed to continued professional development and strive to always educate myself. I love learning from as many different people as possible and aim to keep on top of the latest evidence in the field of women’s pelvic health.

Chronic Disease Management Plans

CDM referrals are very welcome.

Your GP may arrange for your referral paperwork to be sent directly to our clinic, or you can bring it to your appointment. Under the CDM program, GPs can refer clients for a maximum of five Allied Health visits per calendar year to manage chronic conditions. Medicare then subsidises the cost of the appointments that occur under the referral, leaving you with a gap to pay that is significantly less than the full price.

A chronic condition is one that has been present for over six months; many women have been struggling with pelvic pain or incontinence for periods that surpass this. If you are considering booking with us to assess a chronic presentation, it is well worth a trip to the GP to discuss a CDM referral before your appointment.

Mastitis and blocked ducts are part of a
spectrum of inflammatory breast conditions
that occur commonly during breastfeeding.

Early recognition and prompt management are important, as inflammation can progress quickly if left untreated.

Symptoms

You may be experiencing an inflammatory breast condition if you notice:

Localised breast pain, tenderness or swelling

Redness or warmth over part of the breast

A painful lump or firm area in the breast

A feeling of fullness or tension that doesn’t ease after feeding

Feeling generally unwell or run down

As inflammation progresses, symptoms may include fever, chills or flu-like feelings. Early support is important, as untreated inflammation can worsen and lead to infection or abscess formation.

How physiotherapy can help

Women’s health physiotherapy can assist by addressing local inflammation and supporting tissue recovery.

Treatment may include:

Therapeutic ultrasound
to reduce pain, inflammation and swelling

Lymphatic drainage techniques
to assist fluid movement and speed up recovery

Education and guidance
particularly around handling your breast and managing at home

Ultrasound is typically used daily over a short period (often 1–3 days) until symptoms begin to settle. Early treatment tends to lead to faster improvement.

Home management

Alongside physiotherapy and lactation support, the following strategies are recommended:

Continue
physiological feeding (feeding on demand).
Mastitis does not make your milk unsafe, and there is no need to stop feeding on the affected side or to increase feeds to ‘remove the blockage’.

Use
cold packs
after feeds for comfort and to reduce inflammation

Take
anti-inflammatory medication
if appropriate (seek advice from your pharmacist or GP)

Stay well-hydrated

Rest as much as possible and accept help where you can

Please avoid

Firm or deep breast massage

Massaging towards the nipple

Wearing tight or underwire bras

Trying to “clear a blocked duct” through repeated pumping or feeding

Ignoring symptoms in the hope they resolve on their own

These approaches can increase inflammation and worsen symptoms.

Next steps

If you notice early signs of breast inflammation,
prompt support is important

Physiotherapy can help reduce inflammation and support recovery, particularly when treatment begins early. Please seek urgent medical review if you experience fever, chills, rapidly worsening symptoms, or no improvement within 24–48 hours.

If you experience onset of symptoms on a weekend or public holiday, please still give me a call
- mastitis won’t wait until Monday!

Same day in person treatment with therapeutic ultrasound is highly prioritised (home visits can often be negotiated). However, as a sole clinician, there may be times when I’m away or unable to see you in person. If this is the case,  phone advice for home management will be given and I’ll refer you to another clinician who may be able to treat you same day.

During your appointment

Group Exercise Classes

‘Mini & Me’ Post Partum Exercise Classes

Mini & Me’ classes run
Tuesday mornings at the Nuriootpa Netball Clubrooms at 9:00am.
Classes go for 45 minutes, and run in 10 week blocks (in line with the SA school terms).

Classes are small in order to ensure an appropriate physio to client ratio so that everyone receives the supervision and attention they deserve. Classes focus on postpartum rehabilitation particularly of the core, pelvic floor and postural control muscles.

Women from 6 weeks postpartum are welcome to attend, given they have had a Pre-Class Assessment 1:1 appointment. Classes are suitable and beneficial no matter what the birth of your baby or babies was like (whether vaginal or cesarean deliveries).

Women up to 12 months postpartum get the most out of Mini & Me, however you are welcome to attend if your babies are now more like toddlers or kindy kids and you’re looking for a gentle and pelvic floor focused workout! We commonly say ‘post-partum is for life’ - it’s always important to look after your pelvic floor and to find ways to exercise that are enjoyable and accessible!

‘Mini & Me’ classes can be attended for a once off block as a way for you to gradually return to more high impact exercise in your post-partum journey (or you might love it so much you want to keep going!) ‘Mini & Me’ is a great rehabilitation tool to get you back to running, the gym, netball or any other high impact sport or activity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What can I expect during my first visit?

Before your appointment, please fill out the ‘New Client Form’ included in your booking confirmation and appointment reminder email.

Your appointment will include a comprehensive discussion around the history of your concerns as well as your goals and expectations of treatment, followed by a physical assessment which may include an internal examination if you are presenting with pelvic floor related concerns. Treatment will be tailored to your individual needs and assessment findings.

Do I need a referral?

No, you don't need a referral to see me for pelvic health physiotherapy. You are very welcome to bring any relevant letters or documentation from other health professionals to your appointment.

Do I have to have an internal vaginal examination?

An interval vaginal examination is a useful tool that is often used to assess the pelvic floor. In fact, it is considered ‘gold standard’ for determining the strength and tone of the pelvic floor muscles.

You absolutely do not have to have an internal vaginal examination if you do not want to - whether you would rather complete it another day, or not at all, your reasons are valid. There are other options for assessment that we can discuss.

What if I have my period?

Whether we go ahead with a vaginal examination is completely up to you and your level of comfort. It is routine for us to go ahead with a vaginal examination rain, hail, period or shine, however that choice is up to you and how you’re feeling!

Are you qualified to perform digital rectal examinations?

Yes, I have completed training in order to assess anorectal conditions in females.

Do you use a speculum?

No. You may have had a speculum (metal device that holds the walls of the vagina apart) used during medical assessments and procedures. This is not used during digital vaginal examinations.

Is there anything specific I need to do to prepare for an internal examination?

No, not at all. Please do not feel the need to do anything different than what you normally do within your personal hygiene and body hair maintenance routine!

What do I need to bring to my first appointment?

Please bring any relevant paperwork with you (e.g. referrals, letters, reports, scan results), as well as a list of current medications. Some women find it beneficial to write down a list of questions or concerns to bring up during their first appointment - please feel free to do this!

How many appointments will I need?

This will depend on a number of factors such as your unique presentation, individual factors, expectations and goals as well as your body’s response to treatment. A plan for treatment will be discussed during your initial assessment.
