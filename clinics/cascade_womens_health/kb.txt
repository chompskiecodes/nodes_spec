# Cascade Women's Health

Cascade Women's Health is a women's health and pelvic floor physiotherapy clinic located in Tanunda in the Barossa Valley, South Australia. No referral is required.

## PRACTITIONER

Mel Clayfield — Owner & Lead Physiotherapist
Bachelor of Health Sciences/Master of Physiotherapy, Flinders University 2019

Mel is a women's health and pelvic floor physiotherapist with experience across the public health system, not-for-profit organisations, and private practice. She specialises in pregnancy, postpartum recovery, pelvic pain, and pelvic floor concerns.

### Abdominal Scars

Treatment for scars causing:
- Pain, discomfort or pulling around the scar
- Increased or decreased sensitivity
- Dissatisfaction with look or feel of the scar
- Difficulty connecting to deep core muscles
- Inability to fully expand ribs or abdomen with deep inhale

Scar and abdominal release techniques including myofascial release and cupping treat: lower or vertical cesarean scars, abdominoplasty scars, abdominal hysterectomy scars, laparoscopic surgical scars (endometriosis, gall bladder, appendectomy, weight loss surgery etc), oncology scars, hernia repair scars (no releases directly over mesh), bowel resection scars, appendectomy scars, and other abdominal surgical scars.

### Mastitis & Blocked Ducts

Mastitis and blocked ducts are part of a spectrum of inflammatory breast conditions that occur commonly during breastfeeding. Early recognition and prompt management are important.

Symptoms:
- Localised breast pain, tenderness or swelling
- Redness or warmth over part of the breast
- A painful lump or firm area in the breast
- A feeling of fullness or tension that doesn't ease after feeding
- Feeling generally unwell or run down
- As inflammation progresses: fever, chills or flu-like feelings

How physiotherapy can help:
- Therapeutic ultrasound — to reduce pain, inflammation and swelling
- Lymphatic drainage techniques — to assist fluid movement and speed up recovery
- Education and guidance — around handling the breast and managing at home

Ultrasound is typically used daily over a short period (often 1–3 days) until symptoms begin to settle.

Same day in-person treatment with therapeutic ultrasound is highly prioritised. Home visits can often be negotiated. If unable to see you in person, phone advice will be given and referral to another clinician arranged.

## GROUP CLASSES

### Mini & Me Post Partum Exercise Classes

Classes run Tuesday mornings at the Nuriootpa Netball Clubrooms at 9:00am. Classes go for 45 minutes and run in 10-week blocks (in line with SA school terms). Classes are small for appropriate supervision. They focus on postpartum rehabilitation particularly of the core, pelvic floor and postural control muscles. Women from 6 weeks postpartum are welcome, provided they have had a Pre-Class Assessment 1:1 appointment. Classes are suitable for both vaginal and cesarean deliveries. Women up to 12 months postpartum get the most out of Mini & Me, but classes are open to those whose babies are now toddlers or kindy kids.

### Hydrotherapy Classes

Held Tuesday mornings (10:30am and 11:00am) and Thursday afternoons (2:00pm and 4:00pm) at the purpose-built Barossa Village Hydrotherapy Pool in Nuriootpa. Small group classes suitable for those managing chronic pain, arthritis, orthopaedic injuries, reduced mobility, or those looking for a gentle and supportive way to move their body. The warm water environment allows for low-impact exercise to help improve strength, mobility, confidence and overall function.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What can I expect during my first visit?
Before your appointment, please fill out the New Client Form included in your booking confirmation email. Your appointment will include a comprehensive discussion around the history of your concerns and your goals, followed by a physical assessment which may include an internal examination if presenting with pelvic floor concerns. Treatment is tailored to your individual needs.

Do I need a referral?
No referral is needed for pelvic health physiotherapy.

Do I have to have an internal vaginal examination?
No. An internal vaginal examination is useful and considered gold standard for assessing the pelvic floor, but you do not have to have one if you don't want to. Other assessment options are available.

What if I have my period?
Whether to proceed with a vaginal examination during your period is entirely your choice and based on your comfort level.

Are you qualified to perform digital rectal examinations?
Yes. Mel has completed training to assess anorectal conditions in females.

Do you use a speculum?
No. A speculum is not used during digital vaginal examinations.

Is there anything specific I need to do to prepare for an internal examination?
No. No special preparation is needed beyond your normal personal hygiene routine.

What do I need to bring to my first appointment?
Any relevant paperwork (referrals, letters, reports, scan results) and a list of current medications. You may also like to write down any questions or concerns to bring up.

How many appointments will I need?
This depends on your unique presentation, individual factors, expectations and goals, and your body's response to treatment. A treatment plan will be discussed during your initial assessment.

How much does an appointment cost?
See the Fees section above.

Will private health or Medicare cover the cost of my appointment?
Private health coverage varies by fund and level of cover. HICAPS codes are listed in the Fees section above. Medicare will cover part of your fee if you have a Chronic Disease Management Plan — speak to your GP if your symptoms have been chronic (over 6 months).

Can I bring my baby or child?
Absolutely. There is space for a pram/capsule within the clinic room. Feel free to bring something to keep a toddler busy, and to feed, change nappies, or do whatever is needed.

Do you assess and treat paediatrics or men?
Currently, no.

Do you fit pessaries?
Yes. Mel has training in silicone vaginal pessaries for management of pelvic organ prolapse and stress urinary incontinence. Book an initial assessment to see if a pessary is appropriate.
