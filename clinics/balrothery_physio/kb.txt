## BACK PAIN AND SCIATICA

Back pain is one of the most prevalent conditions, affecting people of all ages. It can result from poor posture, muscle strains, disc issues, or more complex conditions like sciatica, where the sciatic nerve becomes irritated or compressed. We provide customised treatment plans to address conditions like herniated discs, spinal stenosis, and muscle imbalances. Using a combination of manual therapy, strengthening exercises, and education on proper body mechanics, we aim to alleviate pain, enhance mobility, and support long-term back health.

## NECK PAIN AND HEADACHES

Neck pain is a common issue, often associated with poor posture, muscle strain, or injuries such as whiplash. Tension in the neck can also lead to headaches, including cervicogenic headaches and migraines. Our treatments focus on relieving muscle tightness, improving posture, and restoring mobility. Through manual therapy, targeted exercises, and ergonomic advice, we address the root causes of neck pain and associated headaches, helping to reduce discomfort, prevent future issues, and improve overall neck function.

---

## KNEE PAIN

Knee pain can arise from a variety of causes, including sports injuries, arthritis, and overuse. Common conditions we treat include meniscal tears, ligament injuries such as ACL or MCL tears, patellofemoral pain syndrome, and osteoarthritis. Our approach involves a thorough assessment to identify the underlying cause, followed by a personalised rehabilitation plan that may include strengthening surrounding muscles, improving joint mobility, and manual therapy to reduce pain and promote healing.

---

## HIP PAIN

Hip pain can limit your ability to move comfortably and may be caused by muscle imbalances, hip labral tears, bursitis, or osteoarthritis. We assess the hip joint to identify the cause of pain and develop a tailored treatment plan. Our therapy focuses on reducing inflammation, improving flexibility and strength, and restoring joint mobility — whether managing chronic hip osteoarthritis or recovering from injuries.

---

## SHOULDER PAIN

Shoulder pain can occur due to injury, overuse, or poor posture. Common conditions include rotator cuff tears, shoulder impingement, frozen shoulder (adhesive capsulitis), and tendinitis. We offer comprehensive treatment plans that focus on restoring strength, improving range of motion, and addressing muscle imbalances. Our manual therapy techniques and tailored exercise programs help relieve pain, enhance mobility, and prevent recurrence.

---

## ELBOW, WRIST AND HAND PAIN

Pain in the elbow, wrist, or hand can be debilitating, affecting your ability to perform everyday tasks. Common conditions include tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis), golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis), carpal tunnel syndrome, and De Quervain's tenosynovitis. Our physiotherapy treatments aim to reduce pain, restore mobility, and improve strength through manual techniques, therapeutic exercises, and ergonomic modifications.

---

## FOOT AND ANKLE PAIN

Foot and ankle pain can result from plantar fasciitis, Achilles tendinopathy, ankle sprains, or fractures — whether from overuse, injury, or chronic conditions like flat feet or arthritis. We develop a personalised rehabilitation plan including improving strength and flexibility, addressing biomechanical issues, and restoring proper foot and ankle alignment.

---

## SPORTS INJURIES

Sports injuries can affect athletes at all levels. Common injuries include ligament sprains, muscle strains, fractures, and overuse injuries like tendinitis or stress fractures. We provide specialised treatment and rehabilitation programs tailored to your specific sport and injury, focusing on accelerating recovery, restoring function, and preventing future injuries.

## PRE- AND POST-OPERATIVE REHABILITATION

Pre- and post-operative rehabilitation is essential for optimising recovery after surgery. Common surgeries we support include joint replacements (hip, knee, shoulder), ligament reconstructions (such as ACL surgery), and spinal surgeries. Before surgery, we strengthen muscles and improve mobility. After surgery, we create a personalised plan to reduce pain, restore movement, and guide you back to normal activities.

## ARTHRITIS AND JOINT PAIN

### About Arthritis and Joint Pain

Arthritis and joint pain can severely impact mobility and quality of life. Common forms include osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and gout. Our physiotherapy approach focuses on reducing pain, improving joint flexibility, and strengthening surrounding muscles to support and stabilise the joints.

We provide personalised exercise programs, manual therapy, and lifestyle advice to help manage symptoms, maintain joint health, and improve daily function — empowering you to stay active despite arthritis.

## BIKE FITTING

### About the Service

Physiotherapist-led bike fitting combining biomechanics expertise with cutting-edge motion analysis technology. Performed by Sean Landers, Chartered Physiotherapist and accredited Bike Fitter with the IBFI. Sean has worked with many top amateur and professional cyclists and has extensive experience coaching at international and domestic level.

### What to Expect from Your Bike Fit

#### 1. Initial Discussion

Discussion around your goals, experience, and needs from a bike fit. We review relevant injury history and any factors that may influence how we fit you to the bike.

#### 2. Physiotherapy Assessment

A cycling-specific physiotherapy assessment analysing mobility, strength, asymmetries, and movement control that directly influence power and comfort.

#### 3. Assessment of Current Bike Set-Up

Precise review of your current bike measurements, contact points, and component selection to establish a baseline.

#### 4. Assessment of Current Riding Position

Dynamic on-bike analysis of joint angles, symmetry, stability, and pedalling mechanics under load using GaitOn motion capture analysis.

#### 5. Adjustment to Riding Position

Targeted adjustments to saddle position, cleat alignment, reach, and drop to optimise power output, control, and aerodynamics.

#### 6. Reassessment and Re-Adjustment

Each positional change is immediately reassessed to evaluate its effect on joint angles, movement efficiency, and stability. Fine adjustments balance optimal power output and aerodynamics with comfort and fatigue resistance.

#### 7. Repeat as Necessary

The process is repeated until the most efficient and repeatable position is achieved within your physical and equipment limits.

#### 8. Fit New Components

Any new components needed are fitted on the day so you leave with a bike ready to ride.

#### 9. Analysis and Fit Report

A detailed before-and-after fit report is provided and emailed after the session, including joint angles, step-by-step changes made, and rationale for each step.

## DRY NEEDLING

Dry needling is a targeted technique using thin, sterile needles to alleviate muscle pain and tension by stimulating trigger points and enhancing blood flow. Ideal for chronic pain, muscle tightness, and sports-related injuries, it promotes faster recovery and improved mobility.
